📋 Seventh Grade ELA Standards

Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) - Seventh Grade ELA

Reading Literary Text (ELAGSE7RL)

  • ELAGSE7RL1: Cite several pieces of textual evidence to support analysis of what the text says explicitly as well as inferences drawn from the text
  • ELAGSE7RL2: Determine a theme or central idea of a text and analyze its development over the course of the text; provide an objective summary of the text
  • ELAGSE7RL3: Analyze how particular elements of a story or drama interact (e.g., how setting shapes the characters or plot)
  • ELAGSE7RL4: Determine the meaning of words and phrases as they are used in a text, including figurative and connotative meanings; analyze the impact of rhymes and other repetitions of sounds on a specific verse or stanza of a poem or section of a story or drama
  • ELAGSE7RL5: Analyze how a drama's or poem's form or structure contributes to its meaning
  • ELAGSE7RL6: Analyze how an author develops and contrasts the points of view of different characters or narrators in a text
  • ELAGSE7RL7: Compare and contrast a written story, drama, or poem to its audio, filmed, staged, or multimedia version, analyzing the effects of techniques unique to each medium
  • ELAGSE7RL9: Compare and contrast a fictional portrayal of a time, place, or character and a historical account of the same period as a means of understanding how authors of fiction use or alter history
  • ELAGSE7RL10: By the end of the year, read and comprehend literature, including stories, dramas, and poems, in the grades 6-8 text complexity band proficiently

Reading Informational Text (ELAGSE7RI)

  • ELAGSE7RI1: Cite several pieces of textual evidence to support analysis of what the text says explicitly as well as inferences drawn from the text
  • ELAGSE7RI2: Determine two or more central ideas in a text and analyze their development over the course of the text; provide an objective summary of the text
  • ELAGSE7RI3: Analyze the interactions between individuals, events, and ideas in a text
  • ELAGSE7RI4: Determine the meaning of words and phrases as they are used in a text, including figurative, connotative, and technical meanings; analyze the impact of a specific word choice on meaning and tone
  • ELAGSE7RI5: Analyze the structure an author uses to organize a text, including how the major sections contribute to the whole and to the development of the ideas
  • ELAGSE7RI6: Determine an author's point of view or purpose in a text and analyze how the author distinguishes his or her position from that of others
  • ELAGSE7RI7: Compare and contrast a text to an audio, video, or multimedia version of the text, analyzing each medium's portrayal of the subject
  • ELAGSE7RI8: Trace and evaluate the argument and specific claims in a text, assessing whether the reasoning is sound and the evidence is relevant and sufficient to support the claims
  • ELAGSE7RI9: Analyze how two or more authors writing about the same topic shape their presentations of key information by emphasizing different evidence or advancing different interpretations of facts
  • ELAGSE7RI10: By the end of the year, read and comprehend literary nonfiction in the grades 6-8 text complexity band proficiently

Writing (ELAGSE7W)

  • ELAGSE7W1: Write arguments to support claims with clear reasons and relevant evidence
  • ELAGSE7W2: Write informative/explanatory texts to examine a topic and convey ideas, concepts, and information through the selection, organization, and analysis of relevant content
  • ELAGSE7W3: Write narratives to develop real or imagined experiences or events using effective technique, relevant descriptive details, and well-structured event sequences
  • ELAGSE7W4: Produce clear and coherent writing in which the development, organization, and style are appropriate to task, purpose, and audience
  • ELAGSE7W5: With some guidance and support from peers and adults, develop and strengthen writing as needed by planning, revising, editing, rewriting, or trying a new approach
  • ELAGSE7W6: Use technology, including the Internet, to produce and publish writing and link to and cite sources as well as to interact and collaborate with others
  • ELAGSE7W7: Conduct short research projects to answer a question, drawing on several sources and generating additional related, focused questions for further research and investigation
  • ELAGSE7W8: Gather relevant information from multiple print and digital sources, using search terms effectively; assess the credibility and accuracy of each source; and quote or paraphrase the data and conclusions of others while avoiding plagiarism and following a standard format for citation
  • ELAGSE7W9: Draw evidence from literary or informational texts to support analysis, reflection, and research
  • ELAGSE7W10: Write routinely over extended time frames and shorter time frames for a range of discipline-specific tasks, purposes, and audiences

Speaking and Listening (ELAGSE7SL)

  • ELAGSE7SL1: Engage effectively in a range of collaborative discussions with diverse partners on grade 7 topics, texts, and issues, building on others' ideas and expressing their own clearly
  • ELAGSE7SL2: Analyze the main ideas and supporting details presented in diverse media and formats and explain how the ideas clarify a topic, text, or issue under study
  • ELAGSE7SL3: Delineate a speaker's argument and specific claims, evaluating the soundness of the reasoning and the relevance and sufficiency of the evidence
  • ELAGSE7SL4: Present claims and findings, emphasizing salient points in a focused, coherent manner with pertinent descriptions, facts, details, and examples; use appropriate eye contact, adequate volume, and clear pronunciation
  • ELAGSE7SL5: Include multimedia components and visual displays in presentations to clarify claims and findings and emphasize salient points
  • ELAGSE7SL6: Adapt speech to a variety of contexts and tasks, demonstrating command of formal English when indicated or appropriate

Language (ELAGSE7L)

  • ELAGSE7L1: Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English grammar and usage when writing or speaking
  • ELAGSE7L2: Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English capitalization, punctuation, and spelling when writing
  • ELAGSE7L3: Use knowledge of language and its conventions when writing, speaking, reading, or listening
  • ELAGSE7L4: Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words and phrases based on grade 7 reading and content
  • ELAGSE7L5: Demonstrate understanding of figurative language, word relationships, and nuances in word meanings
  • ELAGSE7L6: Acquire and use accurately grade-appropriate general academic and domain-specific words and phrases

📖 Teacher Background Knowledge

Seventh Grade Literacy Focus

Seventh grade builds on sixth grade foundations with increased emphasis on analysis and evaluation. Key shifts include citing SEVERAL pieces of evidence, analyzing how elements INTERACT, and evaluating the SOUNDNESS of reasoning. Students move from identifying to analyzing and evaluating.

Key Progression: 6th grade = cite evidence, identify; 7th grade = cite SEVERAL pieces of evidence, ANALYZE interactions, EVALUATE reasoning

Analyzing Interactions

Story Element Interactions

Seventh graders analyze HOW elements interact, not just identify them:

  • How does setting shape character? (time period influences values)
  • How does setting shape plot? (isolated location creates conflict)
  • How does character drive plot? (choices create consequences)
  • How does conflict reveal character? (pressure shows true nature)

Analyzing Point of View Development

Beyond Identification to Analysis

6th Grade 7th Grade
Explain how author develops narrator's POV Analyze how author develops AND CONTRASTS different characters' or narrators' POV

Questions to guide analysis:

  • How does each character/narrator see the same event differently?
  • What techniques does the author use to show contrast?
  • Why does the author include multiple perspectives?
  • How does contrasting viewpoints affect the reader's understanding?

Evaluating Arguments

Sound Reasoning and Sufficient Evidence

Seventh graders evaluate arguments more rigorously:

  • Sound Reasoning: Is the logic valid? Are there logical fallacies?
  • Relevant Evidence: Does the evidence actually relate to the claim?
  • Sufficient Evidence: Is there enough evidence to support the claim?

Common Logical Fallacies to Teach

  • Ad Hominem: Attacking the person instead of the argument
  • Bandwagon: Everyone does it, so it must be right
  • False Cause: Assuming one event caused another
  • Either/Or: Presenting only two options when more exist

Comparing Fiction to History

Historical Fiction Analysis

Seventh grade standard: Compare fictional portrayal to historical account to understand how authors use or alter history.

  • What historical elements are accurate?
  • What has the author changed or invented?
  • Why might the author have made these choices?
  • How does fiction help us understand history differently than nonfiction?

Analyzing Form and Structure

Poetry and Drama Structure

  • Poetry: How does the form (sonnet, free verse, haiku) contribute to meaning?
  • Drama: How do acts, scenes, and stage directions contribute to meaning?
  • Sound Devices: How do rhyme, repetition, and rhythm affect meaning?

📝 Lesson Ideas

Analyzing Element Interactions

Setting Shapes Character Lesson

  1. Identify the setting's key features (time period, location, social context)
  2. Analyze how setting influences character's beliefs, values, opportunities
  3. Find evidence of setting's impact on character's actions/choices
  4. Write analysis: "The setting shapes [character] by..."

Contrasting Points of View

Multiple Narrator Analysis

  • Use texts with multiple narrators (Wonder, The Westing Game)
  • Chart each narrator's perspective on key events
  • Analyze techniques author uses to distinguish voices
  • Discuss why author chose multiple perspectives

Evaluating Arguments

Argument Evaluation Protocol

  1. Identify the main claim
  2. List the reasons and evidence
  3. Evaluate: Is reasoning sound? Is evidence relevant? Sufficient?
  4. Identify any logical fallacies
  5. Write evaluation with specific examples

Fiction vs. History

Historical Fiction Comparison

Pair historical fiction with primary/secondary sources:

  • Read historical fiction selection
  • Research the actual historical events/period
  • Compare: What's accurate? What's changed?
  • Analyze: Why might author have altered history?

🔍 Close Reading and Text Analysis

Element Interaction Investigation

Essential Question

How do setting, character, and plot interact to create meaning?

Investigation Protocol

  1. Select a complex text (The Giver, Roll of Thunder)
  2. Map setting features and their effects
  3. Trace how setting shapes character choices
  4. Analyze how character choices drive plot
  5. Write analytical essay on interactions

Author Comparison Investigation

Essential Question

How do two authors presenting the same information shape their presentations differently?

Analysis Questions

  • What evidence does each author emphasize?
  • What different interpretations do they advance?
  • How do word choices reveal perspective?
  • Why might authors present differently?

Media Comparison Investigation

Text to Film Analysis

  1. Read a story/drama/novel
  2. View film/audio/staged version
  3. Analyze techniques unique to each medium
  4. Compare effects on understanding and emotion
  5. Evaluate which medium is more effective and why

❌ Common Misconceptions

One quote is enough evidence

Reality: Seventh grade requires citing SEVERAL pieces of evidence.

Any evidence supports any claim

Reality: Evidence must be RELEVANT and SUFFICIENT.

Story elements exist separately

Reality: Elements INTERACT to create meaning.
