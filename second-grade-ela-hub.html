📋 Second Grade ELA Standards

Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) - Second Grade ELA

Reading Literary Text (ELAGSE2RL)

  • ELAGSE2RL1: Ask and answer such questions as who, what, where, when, why, and how to demonstrate understanding of key details
  • ELAGSE2RL2: Recount stories, including fables and folktales from diverse cultures, and determine their central message, lesson, or moral
  • ELAGSE2RL3: Describe how characters in a story respond to major events and challenges
  • ELAGSE2RL4: Describe how words and phrases supply rhythm and meaning in a story, poem, or song
  • ELAGSE2RL5: Describe the overall structure of a story, including describing how the beginning introduces the story and the ending concludes the action
  • ELAGSE2RL6: Acknowledge differences in the points of view of characters
  • ELAGSE2RL7: Use information gained from illustrations and words in a text to demonstrate understanding
  • ELAGSE2RL9: Compare and contrast two or more versions of the same story by different authors or from different cultures
  • ELAGSE2RL10: By the end of year, read and comprehend literature in the grades 2-3 text complexity band

Reading Informational Text (ELAGSE2RI)

  • ELAGSE2RI1: Ask and answer questions about who, what, where, when, why, and how
  • ELAGSE2RI2: Identify the main topic of a multi-paragraph text and the focus of specific paragraphs
  • ELAGSE2RI3: Describe the connection between a series of historical events, scientific ideas, or steps
  • ELAGSE2RI4: Determine the meaning of words and phrases in a text relevant to a grade 2 topic
  • ELAGSE2RI5: Know and use various text features to locate key facts or information
  • ELAGSE2RI6: Identify the main purpose of a text, including what the author wants to answer, explain, or describe
  • ELAGSE2RI7: Explain how specific images contribute to and clarify a text
  • ELAGSE2RI8: Describe how reasons support specific points the author makes in a text
  • ELAGSE2RI9: Compare and contrast the most important points presented by two texts on the same topic
  • ELAGSE2RI10: By the end of year, read and comprehend informational texts in the grades 2-3 text complexity band

Reading Foundational Skills (ELAGSE2RF)

  • ELAGSE2RF3: Know and apply grade-level phonics and word analysis skills in decoding words
  • ELAGSE2RF4: Read with sufficient accuracy and fluency to support comprehension

Writing (ELAGSE2W)

  • ELAGSE2W1: Write opinion pieces introducing the topic, stating an opinion, supplying reasons, using linking words, and providing a conclusion
  • ELAGSE2W2: Write informative/explanatory texts introducing a topic, using facts and definitions, and providing a conclusion
  • ELAGSE2W3: Write narratives recounting a well-elaborated event or sequence of events with details
  • ELAGSE2W5: With guidance, focus on a topic and strengthen writing through revision and editing
  • ELAGSE2W6: With guidance, use digital tools to produce and publish writing
  • ELAGSE2W7: Participate in shared research and writing projects
  • ELAGSE2W8: Recall information from experiences or gather information from provided sources

Speaking and Listening (ELAGSE2SL)

  • ELAGSE2SL1: Participate in collaborative conversations with diverse partners
  • ELAGSE2SL2: Recount or describe key ideas or details from text read aloud or information presented
  • ELAGSE2SL3: Ask and answer questions about what a speaker says to clarify, gather information
  • ELAGSE2SL4: Tell a story or recount an experience with appropriate facts and details
  • ELAGSE2SL5: Create audio recordings of stories or poems; add drawings or visual displays
  • ELAGSE2SL6: Produce complete sentences when appropriate to task and situation

Language (ELAGSE2L)

  • ELAGSE2L1: Demonstrate command of standard English grammar and usage
  • ELAGSE2L2: Demonstrate command of capitalization, punctuation, and spelling conventions
  • ELAGSE2L3: Use knowledge of language and its conventions when writing and speaking
  • ELAGSE2L4: Determine or clarify meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words
  • ELAGSE2L5: Demonstrate understanding of word relationships and nuances
  • ELAGSE2L6: Use words and phrases acquired through conversations, reading, and responding to texts

Science of Reading Standards

Phonics and Word Recognition

  • Distinguish long and short vowels when reading regularly spelled one-syllable words
  • Know spelling-sound correspondences for additional common vowel teams
  • Decode regularly spelled two-syllable words with long vowels
  • Decode words with common prefixes and suffixes
  • Identify words with inconsistent but common spelling-sound correspondences
  • Recognize and read grade-appropriate irregularly spelled words

Fluency

  • Read on-level text with purpose and understanding
  • Read on-level text orally with accuracy, appropriate rate, and expression
  • Use context to confirm or self-correct word recognition and understanding, rereading as necessary

Vocabulary Development

  • Use sentence-level context as a clue to meaning
  • Determine meaning of new word formed when a known prefix is added
  • Use a known root word as a clue to meaning of unknown word
  • Use knowledge of individual words to predict meaning of compound words

📖 Teacher Background Knowledge

Second Grade Literacy Development

Second grade marks a pivotal transition as students shift from "learning to read" to "reading to learn." Students consolidate foundational skills while developing more sophisticated comprehension strategies. The Science of Reading emphasizes continued systematic phonics instruction alongside building knowledge and vocabulary.

Phonics Scope and Sequence

Second Grade Phonics Focus:

  1. Review and extend vowel patterns (ai, ay, ee, ea, oa, ow)
  2. Variant vowel patterns (oo, ou, ow, oi, oy, au, aw)
  3. R-controlled vowels (ar, or, er, ir, ur)
  4. Silent letter combinations (kn, wr, gn, mb)
  5. Three-letter blends (str, spr, scr)
  6. Multisyllable words and syllable types
  7. Common prefixes (un-, re-, pre-, dis-)
  8. Common suffixes (-ful, -less, -ly, -er, -est)

Fluency Expectations

Second Grade Fluency Benchmarks

  • Beginning of Year: 50-70 WCPM
  • Middle of Year: 70-90 WCPM
  • End of Year: 90-100+ WCPM

Focus on prosody (expression, phrasing, attention to punctuation) alongside rate and accuracy.

Comprehension Development

Second graders develop more sophisticated comprehension through:

  • Asking and answering questions about text
  • Determining central message and main idea
  • Describing character responses to events
  • Making connections (text-to-self, text-to-text, text-to-world)
  • Understanding text structure (fiction and nonfiction)

Writing Development

Second grade writers should produce:

  • Opinion pieces with reasons supported by linking words
  • Informative texts with facts, definitions, and concluding statements
  • Narratives with well-elaborated events, details, and temporal words
  • Multiple paragraphs on related topics

📝 Ready-to-Use Lessons

Phonics Lessons

Vowel Teams (ai, ay)

Objective: Students decode words with ai and ay vowel patterns.

  1. Introduce the pattern: ai in the middle, ay at the end
  2. Sort words by pattern (rain, train, play, stay)
  3. Build words with letter tiles
  4. Read sentences with vowel team words
  5. Apply in dictation and writing

Comprehension Lessons

Central Message in Fables

Objective: Students determine the central message or moral of a fable.

  1. Read "The Tortoise and the Hare"
  2. Discuss what happens to each character
  3. Ask: "What lesson can we learn from this story?"
  4. Compare to another fable to find similarities in lessons
  5. Students identify morals in independent reading

Writing Lessons

Opinion Writing with Reasons

Objective: Students write opinion pieces with supporting reasons and linking words.

  1. Model opinion statement: "I think recess is the best part of school."
  2. Generate reasons together (exercise, friends, fun)
  3. Introduce linking words: because, and, also
  4. Students draft opinions on self-selected topics
  5. Peer share and revise

🔍 Investigations & Close Reading

Close Reading Strategies

Annotating Text for Second Graders

  • ⭐ Star important information
  • ? Question mark for confusing parts
  • ! Exclamation for surprising facts
  • Circle unfamiliar words
  • Underline the main idea

Text Comparison Investigation

Comparing Cinderella Stories

  1. Read traditional Cinderella
  2. Read cultural variant (Yeh-Shen, Mufaro's Beautiful Daughters)
  3. Complete comparison chart
  4. Discuss similarities and cultural differences
  5. Write about which version students prefer and why

Research Investigation

Animal Research Project

  1. Students choose an animal to research
  2. Gather information from 2-3 sources
  3. Take notes using graphic organizer
  4. Write informational report
  5. Share with class

📚 Vocabulary Instruction

Academic Vocabulary

  • Central message: The main lesson or moral of a story
  • Main idea: What a text is mostly about
  • Supporting details: Facts that tell more about the main idea
  • Compare: Tell how things are alike
  • Contrast: Tell how things are different
  • Sequence: The order events happen
  • Text features: Headings, captions, bold words, diagrams

Word Study Strategies

Morphology Instruction

Second graders learn that words are built from meaningful parts:

  • Prefixes: un- (not), re- (again), pre- (before)
  • Suffixes: -ful (full of), -less (without), -ly (how)
  • Base words: The main part of a word

⚠️ Common Misconceptions

Reading fast means reading well

Reality: Fluency includes accuracy, rate, AND expression. Speed without comprehension is not fluent reading.

Main idea is the same as topic

Reality: Topic is what the text is about (one word or phrase). Main idea is the most important point about the topic (a complete thought).

Every story has a clear moral

Reality: Fables have morals. Other stories have central messages that may be more subtle.
