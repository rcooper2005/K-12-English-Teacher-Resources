🧠 Science of Reading Integration

Phonological Awareness

Systematic instruction in sounds of language, from rhyming to phoneme manipulation, building the foundation for decoding.

Phonics

Explicit, systematic instruction connecting sounds to letters, progressing from simple to complex patterns.

Fluency

Building automaticity through practice, enabling cognitive resources to focus on comprehension.

Vocabulary

Direct instruction and rich language experiences building word knowledge essential for comprehension.

Comprehension

Strategic instruction in understanding text, from literal comprehension to inference and critical analysis.

Writing

Encoding skills that reinforce reading, from spelling patterns to composition and rhetoric.

📋 Standards Frameworks

Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE)

State standards for Reading Literature, Reading Informational Text, Writing, Speaking & Listening, and Language by grade level.

Academic Knowledge & Skills (AKS)

Gwinnett County's curriculum standards that specify what students should know and be able to do at each grade level.

NCTE/IRA Standards

National standards from the National Council of Teachers of English emphasizing literacy development and language arts.

College Board Framework

AP course frameworks for English Language and English Literature defining skills, big ideas, and exam expectations.
