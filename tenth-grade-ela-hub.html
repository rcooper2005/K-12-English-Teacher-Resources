📋 Tenth Grade ELA Standards

Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) - 9th-10th Grade ELA

Tenth grade shares the 9-10 grade band standards with 9th grade but with increased expectations for independence, sophistication, and text complexity at the HIGH END of the band.

Reading Literary Text (ELAGSE9-10RL) - 10th Grade Focus

  • ELAGSE9-10RL1: Cite strong and thorough textual evidence to support analysis of what the text says explicitly as well as inferences drawn from the text
  • ELAGSE9-10RL2: Determine a theme or central idea of a text and analyze in detail its development over the course of the text
  • ELAGSE9-10RL3: Analyze how complex characters develop over the course of a text, interact with other characters, and advance the plot or develop the theme
  • ELAGSE9-10RL4: Determine the meaning of words and phrases as they are used in the text; analyze the cumulative impact of specific word choices on meaning and tone
  • ELAGSE9-10RL5: Analyze how an author's choices concerning how to structure a text, order events, and manipulate time create such effects as mystery, tension, or surprise
  • ELAGSE9-10RL6: Analyze a particular point of view or cultural experience reflected in a work of literature from outside the United States, drawing on a wide reading of world literature
  • ELAGSE9-10RL7: Analyze the representation of a subject or a key scene in two different artistic mediums
  • ELAGSE9-10RL9: Analyze how an author draws on and transforms source material in a specific work
  • ELAGSE9-10RL10: By the end of grade 10, read and comprehend literature at the high end of the grades 9-10 text complexity band independently and proficiently

Reading Informational Text (ELAGSE9-10RI) - 10th Grade Focus

  • ELAGSE9-10RI1: Cite strong and thorough textual evidence to support analysis
  • ELAGSE9-10RI2: Determine a central idea and analyze its development
  • ELAGSE9-10RI3: Analyze how the author unfolds an analysis or series of ideas or events
  • ELAGSE9-10RI4: Analyze the cumulative impact of specific word choices on meaning and tone
  • ELAGSE9-10RI5: Analyze how an author's ideas or claims are developed and refined
  • ELAGSE9-10RI6: Determine author's point of view or purpose; analyze how author uses rhetoric
  • ELAGSE9-10RI7: Analyze various accounts of a subject told in different mediums
  • ELAGSE9-10RI8: Delineate and evaluate argument and claims; identify false statements and fallacious reasoning
  • ELAGSE9-10RI9: Analyze seminal U.S. documents of historical and literary significance
  • ELAGSE9-10RI10: By the end of grade 10, read and comprehend literary nonfiction at the high end of the grades 9-10 text complexity band

Writing (ELAGSE9-10W) - 10th Grade Focus

  • ELAGSE9-10W1: Write arguments to support claims with valid reasoning and relevant and sufficient evidence
  • ELAGSE9-10W2: Write informative/explanatory texts to examine and convey complex ideas clearly and accurately
  • ELAGSE9-10W3: Write narratives with effective technique, well-chosen details, and well-structured event sequences
  • ELAGSE9-10W4-6: Produce clear, coherent writing appropriate to task; develop/strengthen through process; use technology
  • ELAGSE9-10W7-8: Conduct research projects; gather information from authoritative sources; integrate selectively
  • ELAGSE9-10W9-10: Draw evidence from texts; write routinely for various purposes

Science of Reading - 10th Grade Application

Advanced Comprehension at High End of Band

  • Cite strong and thorough evidence with increasing sophistication
  • Analyze complex themes and their detailed development
  • Analyze sophisticated structural choices and their effects
  • Evaluate rhetoric and identify fallacious reasoning
  • Synthesize multiple complex texts

Vocabulary and Language

  • Analyze cumulative impact of word choices
  • Understand nuanced meanings and connotations
  • Use academic and domain-specific vocabulary precisely
  • Write with varied syntax for effect

Text Complexity

  • HIGH END of Lexile Range: 1050L-1335L
  • More complex literary techniques and structures
  • Sophisticated rhetorical analysis
  • Preparation for 11th-12th grade complexity

Gwinnett County Academic Knowledge and Skills (AKS) - 10th Grade ELA

Reading and Literature

  • Cite strong and thorough textual evidence with increasing sophistication
  • Analyze theme development through specific details at advanced level
  • Analyze complex characters and their roles in advancing plot/theme
  • Analyze cumulative impact of word choices independently
  • Analyze structural choices and their effects on reader

Writing

  • Demonstrate mastery of citation formats

Language

  • Use semicolons and colons correctly
  • Vary syntax for meaning, interest, and style

📖 Teacher Background Knowledge

Tenth Grade: Mastery of 9-10 Band

Tenth grade represents the mastery level of the 9-10 grade band. Students should demonstrate independent proficiency with the skills introduced in 9th grade, handling more complex texts and producing more sophisticated analysis and writing. The focus shifts toward preparation for the 11-12 grade band and, for some students, AP courses.

Key Expectation: By end of 10th grade, students should read and comprehend at the HIGH END of the 9-10 text complexity band INDEPENDENTLY and PROFICIENTLY.

Deepening Literary Analysis

More Sophisticated Character Analysis

  • Analyze psychological complexity and motivation
  • Trace character arcs across entire texts
  • Analyze character as vehicle for theme
  • Compare character development across texts

Advanced Theme Analysis

  • Analyze multiple themes within single work
  • Trace theme development across literary movements
  • Connect themes to historical/cultural context
  • Analyze how themes are refined and complicated

World Literature Focus

Cultural Experience in Literature

10th grade often focuses on world literature, analyzing how cultural context shapes literature:

  • European literature (Greek tragedy, Renaissance)
  • African literature (postcolonial perspectives)
  • Asian literature (Japanese, Chinese, Indian traditions)
  • Latin American literature (magical realism)
  • Middle Eastern literature (ancient and modern)

Advanced Rhetorical Analysis

Beyond Basic Appeals

Rhetorical Strategy Definition Effect
Juxtaposition Placing contrasting elements side by side Highlights differences, creates meaning through contrast
Understatement Deliberate minimization for effect Creates irony, draws attention through restraint
Hyperbole Deliberate exaggeration Emphasizes a point, creates humor or urgency
Allusion Reference to other work/event Adds depth, connects to larger ideas

🔍 Close Reading and Text Analysis

World Literature Investigation

Essential Question

How does cultural context shape literary choices and meanings?

Investigation Protocol

  1. Select text from non-U.S. tradition
  2. Research cultural and historical context
  3. Analyze how context shapes elements
  4. Compare to familiar traditions
  5. Present findings with textual evidence

Rhetorical Effectiveness Investigation

Essential Question

How effectively do authors use rhetoric to achieve their purpose?

Analysis Protocol

  1. Identify author's purpose and audience
  2. Analyze rhetorical strategies used
  3. Evaluate effectiveness of choices
  4. Identify any fallacious reasoning
  5. Write evaluation with specific evidence

Theme Comparison Investigation

Essential Question

How do different authors/cultures approach similar themes?

Investigation Steps

  1. Select texts with similar themes from different traditions
  2. Analyze theme development in each
  3. Compare approaches and perspectives
  4. Connect to cultural context
  5. Write comparative analysis
