📋 Ninth Grade ELA Standards

Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) - 9th-10th Grade ELA

Reading Literary Text (ELAGSE9-10RL)

  • ELAGSE9-10RL1: Cite strong and thorough textual evidence to support analysis of what the text says explicitly as well as inferences drawn from the text
  • ELAGSE9-10RL2: Determine a theme or central idea of a text and analyze in detail its development over the course of the text, including how it emerges and is shaped and refined by specific details; provide an objective summary of the text
  • ELAGSE9-10RL3: Analyze how complex characters develop over the course of a text, interact with other characters, and advance the plot or develop the theme
  • ELAGSE9-10RL4: Determine the meaning of words and phrases as they are used in the text, including figurative and connotative meanings; analyze the cumulative impact of specific word choices on meaning and tone
  • ELAGSE9-10RL5: Analyze how an author's choices concerning how to structure a text, order events within it, and manipulate time create such effects as mystery, tension, or surprise
  • ELAGSE9-10RL6: Analyze a particular point of view or cultural experience reflected in a work of literature from outside the United States, drawing on a wide reading of world literature
  • ELAGSE9-10RL7: Analyze the representation of a subject or a key scene in two different artistic mediums
  • ELAGSE9-10RL9: Analyze how an author draws on and transforms source material in a specific work
  • ELAGSE9-10RL10: By the end of grade 9, read and comprehend literature, including stories, dramas, and poems, in the grades 9-10 text complexity band proficiently

Reading Informational Text (ELAGSE9-10RI)

  • ELAGSE9-10RI1: Cite strong and thorough textual evidence to support analysis of what the text says explicitly as well as inferences drawn from the text
  • ELAGSE9-10RI2: Determine a central idea of a text and analyze its development over the course of the text, including how it emerges and is shaped and refined by specific details; provide an objective summary of the text
  • ELAGSE9-10RI3: Analyze how the author unfolds an analysis or series of ideas or events, including the order in which the points are made, how they are introduced and developed, and the connections that are drawn between them
  • ELAGSE9-10RI4: Determine the meaning of words and phrases as they are used in a text, including figurative, connotative, and technical meanings; analyze the cumulative impact of specific word choices on meaning and tone
  • ELAGSE9-10RI5: Analyze in detail how an author's ideas or claims are developed and refined by particular sentences, paragraphs, or larger portions of a text
  • ELAGSE9-10RI6: Determine an author's point of view or purpose in a text and analyze how an author uses rhetoric to advance that point of view or purpose
  • ELAGSE9-10RI7: Analyze various accounts of a subject told in different mediums, determining which details are emphasized in each account
  • ELAGSE9-10RI8: Delineate and evaluate the argument and specific claims in a text, assessing whether the reasoning is valid and the evidence is relevant and sufficient; identify false statements and fallacious reasoning
  • ELAGSE9-10RI9: Analyze seminal U.S. documents of historical and literary significance, including how they address related themes and concepts
  • ELAGSE9-10RI10: By the end of grade 9, read and comprehend literary nonfiction in the grades 9-10 text complexity band proficiently

Writing (ELAGSE9-10W)

  • ELAGSE9-10W1: Write arguments to support claims in an analysis of substantive topics or texts, using valid reasoning and relevant and sufficient evidence
  • ELAGSE9-10W2: Write informative/explanatory texts to examine and convey complex ideas, concepts, and information clearly and accurately through the effective selection, organization, and analysis of content
  • ELAGSE9-10W3: Write narratives to develop real or imagined experiences or events using effective technique, well-chosen details, and well-structured event sequences
  • ELAGSE9-10W4: Produce clear and coherent writing in which the development, organization, and style are appropriate to task, purpose, and audience
  • ELAGSE9-10W5: Develop and strengthen writing as needed by planning, revising, editing, rewriting, or trying a new approach, focusing on addressing what is most significant for a specific purpose and audience
  • ELAGSE9-10W6: Use technology, including the Internet, to produce, publish, and update individual or shared writing products
  • ELAGSE9-10W7: Conduct short as well as more sustained research projects to answer a question or solve a problem
  • ELAGSE9-10W8: Gather relevant information from multiple authoritative print and digital sources, using advanced searches effectively; assess the usefulness of each source in answering the research question; integrate information into the text selectively to maintain the flow of ideas
  • ELAGSE9-10W9: Draw evidence from literary or informational texts to support analysis, reflection, and research
  • ELAGSE9-10W10: Write routinely over extended time frames and shorter time frames for a range of tasks, purposes, and audiences

Speaking and Listening (ELAGSE9-10SL)

  • ELAGSE9-10SL1: Initiate and participate effectively in a range of collaborative discussions with diverse partners on grades 9-10 topics, texts, and issues, building on others' ideas and expressing their own clearly and persuasively
  • ELAGSE9-10SL2: Integrate multiple sources of information presented in diverse media or formats, evaluating the credibility and accuracy of each source
  • ELAGSE9-10SL3: Evaluate a speaker's point of view, reasoning, and use of evidence and rhetoric, identifying any fallacious reasoning or exaggerated or distorted evidence
  • ELAGSE9-10SL4: Present information, findings, and supporting evidence clearly, concisely, and logically such that listeners can follow the line of reasoning
  • ELAGSE9-10SL5: Make strategic use of digital media in presentations to enhance understanding of findings, reasoning, and evidence and to add interest
  • ELAGSE9-10SL6: Adapt speech to a variety of contexts and tasks, demonstrating command of formal English when indicated or appropriate

Language (ELAGSE9-10L)

  • ELAGSE9-10L1: Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English grammar and usage when writing or speaking
  • ELAGSE9-10L2: Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English capitalization, punctuation, and spelling when writing
  • ELAGSE9-10L3: Apply knowledge of language to understand how language functions in different contexts, to make effective choices for meaning or style
  • ELAGSE9-10L4: Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words and phrases based on grades 9-10 reading and content
  • ELAGSE9-10L5: Demonstrate understanding of figurative language, word relationships, and nuances in word meanings
  • ELAGSE9-10L6: Acquire and use accurately general academic and domain-specific words and phrases

📖 Teacher Background Knowledge

The Transition to High School ELA

Ninth grade marks the beginning of high school rigor with significant increases in expectations for analysis, writing, and independent learning. The shift from "strong evidence" to "strong AND thorough evidence" signals deeper, more comprehensive analysis. Students analyze complex characters, sophisticated structures, and rhetorical techniques while engaging with world literature and seminal documents.

Key Progression: Middle School = cite strong evidence; High School = cite STRONG AND THOROUGH evidence, analyze COMPLEX characters, examine how theme EMERGES and is REFINED, analyze CUMULATIVE IMPACT of choices

Analyzing Complex Characters

What Makes a Character "Complex"?

  • Multiple dimensions: Contradictory traits, internal conflicts
  • Development: Changes over the course of the text
  • Motivation: Complex, sometimes unclear motivations
  • Interactions: Relationships that reveal character and advance plot/theme

Analysis Questions

  • How does the character change from beginning to end?
  • What contradictions exist within the character?
  • How do interactions with other characters reveal complexity?
  • How does character development advance plot or theme?

Analyzing How Theme Emerges and is Refined

Theme Development Analysis

Ninth graders analyze not just what the theme IS but HOW it develops:

  • Emerges: How/when does the theme first become apparent?
  • Shaped: How do specific details and events develop the theme?
  • Refined: How does understanding of theme become more nuanced?

Tracking Theme Development

Stage Questions to Ask
Beginning What hints suggest possible themes? What issues are introduced?
Middle How do events and character choices shape understanding of theme?
End How is theme refined or complicated by the conclusion?

Rhetorical Analysis

Introduction to Rhetoric

  • Ethos: Appeals to credibility and character
  • Pathos: Appeals to emotion
  • Logos: Appeals to logic and reason

Rhetorical Devices to Teach

Device Definition Example
Anaphora Repetition at beginning of clauses "We shall fight... We shall fight... We shall fight..."
Antithesis Contrasting ideas in parallel structure "Ask not what your country can do for you—ask what you can do for your country"
Parallelism Similar grammatical structure "I came, I saw, I conquered"
Rhetorical Question Question asked for effect, not answer "What is the cost of freedom?"

Analyzing Cultural Experience

Standard RL6 specifically addresses analyzing point of view or cultural experience in world literature:

📝 Lesson Ideas

Complex Character Analysis

Character Development Tracking

  1. Chart character traits at beginning, middle, and end of text
  2. Identify contradictions within the character
  3. Analyze key interactions and their effects
  4. Connect character development to plot advancement
  5. Connect character development to theme refinement

Theme Development Analysis

Tracking Theme Emergence and Refinement

  1. Identify moments where theme first emerges
  2. Track specific details that shape understanding
  3. Analyze how events complicate or refine theme
  4. Write analysis of theme development with evidence

Rhetorical Analysis Lesson

SOAPSTone + Rhetoric

  1. Apply SOAPSTone to establish context
  2. Identify rhetorical appeals (ethos, pathos, logos)
  3. Analyze specific rhetorical devices
  4. Evaluate effectiveness of rhetoric
  5. Write rhetorical analysis essay

  5. Discuss universal themes vs. cultural specifics

🔍 Close Reading and Text Analysis

Complex Character Investigation

Essential Question

How do complex characters develop and what do they reveal about theme?

Investigation Protocol

  1. Select a complex character from a text
  2. Track development through key scenes
  3. Analyze contradictions and motivations
  4. Connect development to plot and theme
  5. Write analytical essay with strong, thorough evidence

Rhetorical Analysis Investigation

Essential Question

How do authors use rhetoric to advance their purpose?

Analysis Protocol

  1. Establish context (speaker, audience, purpose)
  2. Identify rhetorical appeals
  3. Analyze specific devices and their effects
  4. Evaluate effectiveness
  5. Identify any fallacious reasoning or distorted evidence

Source Transformation Investigation

Essential Question

How do authors draw on and transform source material?

Investigation Steps

  1. Identify the source material (myth, history, earlier work)
  2. Read/research the original source
  3. Analyze what the author borrowed
  4. Analyze how the author transformed the material
  5. Evaluate the effects of transformation

❌ Common Misconceptions

Theme is stated directly

Reality: Theme emerges and is refined through specific details; rarely stated directly.

Complex = likeable

Reality: Complex characters have multiple dimensions, not necessarily positive ones.

Rhetoric = manipulation

Reality: Rhetoric is the art of effective communication; can be used ethically or unethically.
