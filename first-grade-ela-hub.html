📋 First Grade ELA Standards

Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) - First Grade ELA

Reading Literary Text (ELAGSE1RL)

  • ELAGSE1RL1: Ask and answer questions about key details in a text
  • ELAGSE1RL2: Retell stories, including key details, and demonstrate understanding of their central message or lesson
  • ELAGSE1RL3: Describe characters, settings, and major events in a story, using key details
  • ELAGSE1RL4: Identify words and phrases in stories or poems that suggest feelings or appeal to the senses
  • ELAGSE1RL5: Explain major differences between books that tell stories and books that give information
  • ELAGSE1RL6: Identify who is telling the story at various points in a text
  • ELAGSE1RL7: Use illustrations and details in a story to describe its characters, setting, or events
  • ELAGSE1RL9: Compare and contrast the adventures and experiences of characters in stories
  • ELAGSE1RL10: With prompting and support, read prose and poetry of appropriate complexity for grade 1

Reading Informational Text (ELAGSE1RI)

  • ELAGSE1RI1: Ask and answer questions about key details in a text
  • ELAGSE1RI2: Identify the main topic and retell key details of a text
  • ELAGSE1RI3: Describe the connection between two individuals, events, ideas, or pieces of information
  • ELAGSE1RI4: Ask and answer questions to determine or clarify meaning of words and phrases
  • ELAGSE1RI5: Know and use various text features to locate key facts or information
  • ELAGSE1RI6: Distinguish between information provided by pictures and information provided by words
  • ELAGSE1RI7: Use illustrations and details in a text to describe its key ideas
  • ELAGSE1RI8: Identify the reasons an author gives to support points in a text
  • ELAGSE1RI9: Identify basic similarities and differences between two texts on the same topic
  • ELAGSE1RI10: With prompting and support, read informational texts appropriately complex for grade 1

Reading Foundational Skills (ELAGSE1RF)

  • ELAGSE1RF1: Demonstrate understanding of the organization and basic features of print
  • ELAGSE1RF2: Demonstrate understanding of spoken words, syllables, and sounds (phonemes)
  • ELAGSE1RF3: Know and apply grade-level phonics and word analysis skills in decoding words
  • ELAGSE1RF4: Read with sufficient accuracy and fluency to support comprehension

Writing (ELAGSE1W)

  • ELAGSE1W1: Write opinion pieces introducing the topic, stating an opinion, supplying a reason, and providing closure
  • ELAGSE1W2: Write informative/explanatory texts naming a topic, supplying facts, and providing closure
  • ELAGSE1W3: Write narratives recounting two or more appropriately sequenced events
  • ELAGSE1W5: With guidance, focus on a topic, respond to questions and suggestions, and add details
  • ELAGSE1W6: With guidance, use digital tools to produce and publish writing
  • ELAGSE1W7: Participate in shared research and writing projects
  • ELAGSE1W8: With guidance, recall information from experiences or gather from sources

Speaking and Listening (ELAGSE1SL)

  • ELAGSE1SL1: Participate in collaborative conversations with diverse partners about grade 1 topics
  • ELAGSE1SL2: Ask and answer questions about key details in a text read aloud or information presented
  • ELAGSE1SL3: Ask and answer questions about what a speaker says to gather information or clarify
  • ELAGSE1SL4: Describe people, places, things, and events with relevant details, expressing ideas clearly
  • ELAGSE1SL5: Add drawings or other visual displays to descriptions when appropriate
  • ELAGSE1SL6: Produce complete sentences when appropriate to task and situation

Language (ELAGSE1L)

  • ELAGSE1L1: Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English grammar and usage
  • ELAGSE1L2: Demonstrate command of capitalization, punctuation, and spelling conventions
  • ELAGSE1L4: Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown words and multiple-meaning words
  • ELAGSE1L5: With guidance, demonstrate understanding of word relationships and nuances
  • ELAGSE1L6: Use words and phrases acquired through conversations, reading, and responding to texts

Science of Reading Standards

Phonological Awareness

  • Distinguish long from short vowel sounds in spoken single-syllable words
  • Orally produce single-syllable words by blending sounds including consonant blends
  • Isolate and pronounce initial, medial vowel, and final sounds in spoken single-syllable words
  • Segment spoken single-syllable words into their complete sequence of phonemes

Phonics and Word Recognition

  • Know the spelling-sound correspondences for common consonant digraphs (sh, th, ch, wh, ck)
  • Decode regularly spelled one-syllable words
  • Know final -e and common vowel team conventions for representing long vowel sounds
  • Use knowledge that every syllable must have a vowel sound
  • Decode two-syllable words following basic patterns by breaking into syllables
  • Read words with inflectional endings (-s, -ed, -ing)
  • Recognize and read grade-appropriate irregularly spelled words

Fluency

  • Read on-level text with purpose and understanding
  • Read on-level text orally with accuracy, appropriate rate, and expression
  • Use context to confirm or self-correct word recognition and understanding

Vocabulary Development

  • Use sentence-level context as a clue to meaning of words and phrases
  • Use frequently occurring affixes as clues to meaning
  • Identify frequently occurring root words and their inflectional forms

Comprehension

  • Build background knowledge through wide reading
  • Apply comprehension strategies before, during, and after reading
  • Understand story structure and text features
  • Make inferences and draw conclusions from text

📖 Teacher Background Knowledge

Understanding First Grade Literacy Development

First grade marks a critical transition from emergent to early reading. Students move from "learning to read" foundations toward more independent reading while continuing to build foundational skills. The Science of Reading research emphasizes systematic, explicit instruction in phonics alongside rich language experiences.

The Simple View of Reading

Reading Comprehension = Decoding × Language Comprehension

Both components are essential. Strong decoding with weak language comprehension, or vice versa, results in poor reading comprehension. First grade instruction must address both.

Phonics Scope and Sequence for First Grade

Typical First Grade Phonics Progression:

  1. Review short vowels and consonants
  2. Consonant digraphs (sh, ch, th, wh, ck)
  3. Consonant blends (beginning and ending)
  4. Long vowel patterns (CVCe, vowel teams)
  5. R-controlled vowels (ar, or, er, ir, ur)
  6. Soft c and g
  7. Inflectional endings (-s, -es, -ed, -ing)
  8. Two-syllable words

Fluency Development

First Grade Fluency Expectations

  • Beginning of Year: 10-30 WCPM (words correct per minute)
  • Middle of Year: 30-50 WCPM
  • End of Year: 50-80 WCPM

Focus on accuracy first, then rate and expression.

Writing Development in First Grade

First graders move from emergent writing to more conventional forms. They should:

  • Write multiple sentences on a topic
  • Use beginning, middle, and end in narratives
  • Apply phonics knowledge to spell words
  • Use capitals and end punctuation
  • Revise with guidance

High-Frequency Words

First graders should master approximately 100 high-frequency words by year's end. Many are irregular (don't follow phonics patterns) and must be memorized. Practice should include reading in context, not just flash cards.

📝 Ready-to-Use Lessons

Phonics Lessons

Consonant Digraph Introduction (sh, ch, th)

Objective: Students will identify and read words with consonant digraphs.

Materials: Digraph cards, word sorts, decodable text

Procedure:

  1. Introduce the concept: "Two letters that make one sound"
  2. Model each digraph with picture cards and key words
  3. Guide students through a word sort (sh/ch/th)
  4. Practice reading decodable sentences with digraphs
  5. Students apply in writing: dictation sentences

Long Vowel Silent-e (CVCe Pattern)

Objective: Students will decode CVCe words and understand the silent-e rule.

Procedure:

  1. Review CVC words (can, hop, pin)
  2. Add 'e' and model the vowel change (cane, hope, pine)
  3. Practice with word chains: can → cane → cape
  4. Read decodable text featuring CVCe words
  5. Write sentences using CVCe words

Comprehension Lessons

Story Elements with Story Map

Objective: Students will identify and describe characters, setting, and major events.

Procedure:

  1. Before reading: Preview book and make predictions
  2. During reading: Pause to discuss characters, setting, problem
  3. After reading: Complete story map together
  4. Students retell to a partner using story map
  5. Independent practice with leveled reader

Writing Workshop Mini-Lessons

Small Moment Narrative Writing

Objective: Students will write a focused narrative about one small moment.

Procedure:

  1. Model zooming in: "Instead of my whole birthday, just opening one present"
  2. Students brainstorm small moments from their lives
  3. Sketch beginning, middle, end across three pages
  4. Add words to pictures, stretching sounds
  5. Share with writing partners

🔍 Investigations & Close Reading

Close Reading for First Grade

First graders can engage in close reading with short, complex texts read multiple times for different purposes.

Three Reads Protocol

First Read: What is the text about? (Literal comprehension)

Second Read: How does the text work? (Craft and structure)

Third Read: What does the text mean? (Integration and deeper understanding)

Text-Dependent Questions

Question stems for first grade:

  • "What does the author tell us about...?"
  • "How do you know that...? Show me in the text."
  • "What happened first/next/last?"
  • "Why do you think the character...?"
  • "What does this word mean in the story?"

Author's Purpose Investigation

PIE: Persuade, Inform, Entertain

  1. Read several short texts of different types
  2. Introduce PIE anchor chart
  3. Sort texts by author's purpose
  4. Discuss clues that helped identify purpose
  5. Students identify purpose in independent reading

Compare and Contrast Investigation

Two Books, Same Topic

Choose two books on the same topic (e.g., penguins, weather, friendship)

  1. Read both books over several days
  2. Create comparison chart: "Book 1 says... Book 2 says..."
  3. Identify similarities and differences
  4. Discuss which book taught more/was more interesting

📊 Assessment Strategies

Foundational Skills Assessments

DIBELS/Acadience Assessments

  • Phoneme Segmentation Fluency (PSF): Segment words into sounds
  • Nonsense Word Fluency (NWF): Decode unfamiliar words
  • Oral Reading Fluency (ORF): Read grade-level passages

Phonics Assessments

  • Consonant sounds and digraphs
  • Short and long vowel patterns
  • Blends and r-controlled vowels
  • Decoding real and nonsense words

Running Records

First Grade Running Record Guidelines:

  • Use texts at instructional level (90-94% accuracy)
  • Analyze error patterns (visual, meaning, structure)
  • Track self-correction ratio
  • Note fluency and expression
  • Conduct every 2-4 weeks

Writing Assessments

On-Demand Writing Samples

Collect quarterly samples to assess:

  • Message/content development
  • Organization (beginning, middle, end)
  • Use of conventions
  • Spelling development

📚 Vocabulary Instruction

Academic Vocabulary

First Grade Literary Terms

  • Main character: The most important person in the story
  • Setting: Where and when the story happens
  • Problem: The trouble in the story
  • Solution: How the problem gets fixed
  • Retell: Tell the story again in your own words
  • Main idea: What the text is mostly about
  • Detail: A small piece of information

Vocabulary Instruction Strategies

Word Learning Routine

  1. Introduce the word in context
  2. Provide kid-friendly definition
  3. Give examples and non-examples
  4. Have students use the word
  5. Review throughout the week

🎯 Differentiation Strategies

Supporting Struggling Readers

Intervention Strategies

  • Provide additional phonics practice with decodable texts
  • Use multisensory approaches (sand writing, arm tapping)
  • Offer more repetition and practice opportunities
  • Break skills into smaller steps
  • Use visual supports and graphic organizers

ELL Support

Strategies for English Learners

  • Build oral language before reading
  • Use pictures, realia, and gestures
  • Pre-teach vocabulary before reading
  • Provide native language support when possible
  • Allow wait time for responses

⚠️ Common Misconceptions

Phonics Misconceptions

"Silent E makes the vowel say its name"

Better understanding: The silent e signals a long vowel sound in the pattern. The vowel doesn't "say its name" - it makes its long sound.

Guessing from pictures is a good reading strategy

Reality: While pictures support comprehension, skilled readers decode words. Encourage students to look at letters first, then confirm with pictures.

Comprehension Misconceptions

Retelling means listing every event

Better understanding: Retelling captures the most important events and central message, not every detail.

Writing Misconceptions

Correct spelling is most important in first grade writing

Reality: Message and ideas come first. Invented spelling that shows phonetic understanding is developmentally appropriate. Correct spelling develops over time.
